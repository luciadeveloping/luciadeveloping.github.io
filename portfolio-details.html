Another 4X Game

Project information

  • Project date: September 2024 - June 2025
  • Languajes & Technologies: Unity, C#

Description

My bachelor's thesis included this small prototype which models an event-driven 4X game with resource management, exploration, and building mechanics.

Access my Bachelor's Thesis (Spanish Only)

Comments

This short demo was developed as part of my bachelor's thesis, titled "Abstraction and Randomness in 4X Strategy: A Design Approach to Improve User Interaction with Complex Systems," which received a perfect grade of 10. The thesis includes an analysis of 4X games and introduces an innovative event-driven design methodology to address their inherent complexity growth. Furthermore, it outlines a proposed architecture, drawing inspiration from data-driven programming, which then is used to bring the theoretical concepts to life in this functional demo.

Building upon these theoretical underpinnings, the demo itself showcases procedural map generation, which ensures a fresh and unique experience with each playthrough. It also features a unique "Ethos" system, specifically designed to add a layer of emergent narrative and impactful player choice as well as engaging dilemmas that force meaningful decisions, all while navigating the fundamental 4X pillars of exploration and building that allow for strategic expansion and development.

Arcana Empatia

Project information

  • Project date: Ferruary-June, 2021
  • Languajes & Technologies: Unity, C#

Description

A rich narrative game where you play as a mage tasked with helping the people of two warring nations. Use the power of divination to advise your clients or harness the light of celestial bodies to craft powerful trinkets.

Comments

This was the very first game I created, alongside my peers at the time. As is often the case with amateur projects, we underestimated the scope and ended up with a massive game: multiple characters across various storylines—all connected within a larger narrative—complex dialogues that evolved based on your choices, interesting puzzles, and more. I’m proud to say we managed to complete most of what we originally envisioned. The workload was intense, but my team and I were passionate about the project. I took on the roles of game and narrative designer. The game featured a mix of traditional puzzles and "mental" puzzles, where players had to interpret the stars to influence the fate of characters (for more details, check out the GDD, in Spanish only). Overall, it was a challenging yet incredibly rewarding project that will always hold a special place in my heart.

Steam War (Guerra de vapor)

Project information

  • Project date: September-February, 2022
  • Languajes & Technologies: Unity, C#, Xbox Adaptive Controller
  • Project URL: https://github.com/luciadeveloping/steamWar

Description

A strategic card game where you play as a machine that uses its health as a resource to battle enemies. The game is played using only two buttons!

Comments

This game was created early in my college career. I didn’t have much experience back then and this was one of my first “complex” games made using the Unity editor. While the gameplay may feel a bit confusing and not as responsive as I'd like, I believe what stands out most about this project is the design approach. The game was developed for a class focused on accessibility, and it was specifically designed to be played with the Xbox Accessible Controller. With only two buttons (A to interact, D to move on a keyboard) available for gameplay, this presented a fun and unique challenge. I took on the role of game designer and was responsible for implementing most of the game’s features.
